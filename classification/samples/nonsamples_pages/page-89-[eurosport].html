What a game that was, and what an affirming comeback that was from Watford. Arsenal move seventh, level on points with Spurs who are third, and Watford, still without a win this season, stay bottom a point behind Wolves.

Here come Watford again, Doucoure charging through the middle like a buffalo gazelle and finding Sarr down the right. His return cross is perfect and Doucoure meets it perfectly, punching a sidefooter without breaking stride ... straight at Leno! That was so good, but that was suuuuch a chance!

Arsenal win a free-kick 40 yards from goal which, by the standards of the half, amounts to a chance. Obviously Xhaka whacks it at the nearest defender and Watford break, but Sarr can't find a man when he squares into the middle.

You hate to see it.

PENALTY TO ARSENAL! Pereyra runs at Luiz, nips across him into the box, and yerman can't help but bring him down. Of course he can't!

Change for Watford, Janmaat replacing Holebas.

A low cross into the Arsenal box causes minor panic, but Sarr can't profit and they get it away eventually.

The changes might just be helping Arsenal, Nelson finding Aubameyang, who we've not seen in time. He jinks about the right side of the box before playing his man back in; the feet are fast but not fast enough, and Kabasele clears.

Maitland-Niles takes a break from being worked over to get forward, pulling back to Nelson on the edge of the box; he decides to make sure his shot goes on target, in the process sacrificing power and placement. Foster collects easily.

66'

Arsenal are struggling here, so Emery sends on Torreira for Guendouzi in a bid to stem the flow. I'm surprised it's not Ozil who's gone off - I guess Emery wants a clinching goal - and as the crowd boo, Guendouzi gestures at them.

GOAL! Watford 1-2 Arsenal (Cleverley) Ahahahahahahahaha, Arsenal defend like children! Leno takes a short goalkick to Sokratis, who can't master a short pass to Guendouzi on the edge, nor decide the smash it out of danger when the men close in. So Deulofeu intercepts then Cleverley snatches the loose ball and wallops home; how many warnings do Arsenal want?

It's been a quiet start to the half. Both teams know this is over, though if Watford score next we'll have something to enjoy.

48' 

Guendouzi is very likeable - he plays with proper love and edge, as we saw when he couldn't wait to wade into that rumble at the end of the first half. With Tierney, he'll give Arsenal a bit of personality, which they've lacked for quite some time. But in classic Arsenal style, he thinks he's a lot better than he is, which is why we see errors of the type he's made today. One half-decent derby performance is not where it's at.

47' 

Guendouzi gives it away, again, playing a blind pass across his own penalty box and straight to Gray; he's saved by an iffy first touch and a decent challenge from Kolasinac.
