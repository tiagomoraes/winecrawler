After booking a place in the semi-finals, it seems that history might be lining up for the Three Lions...

In their 15th World Cup, England have now reached the semi-finals 3 times. It has been 28 years since they were last here...

At Italy 90 they went out to Germany FR in a famous shootout in the last 4 that started their penalty jinx.

But they broke the jinx against Colombia in the Round of 16. It was their first victory in a penalty shootout in the World Cup after three defeats.

A huge key for them of course has been top scorer and captain Harry Kane, who is leading the race for the adidas Golden Boot with six goals

England have been playing with the freedom of youth, and they are the third-youngest team at the finals. Their average age is just 26 years and 1 month.

England's success in Russia comes on the back of an amazing 2017, which saw them become just the second nation ever to win the U-17 and U-20 World Cups in the same year (after Brazil in 2003).
